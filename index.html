Benefits Education

Use this section as a quick guide to understand common benefit terms and how the medical plans differ.

CDHP/HSA Plan vs. Traditional Plan

HSA Plan / CDHP

The HSA Plan generally has lower monthly employee premiums but higher deductible and out-of-pocket exposure. It is paired with a Health Savings Account when eligibility requirements are met.

  • Lower paycheck deductions.
  • Eligible employees can use an HSA for qualified expenses.
  • County HSA funding and matching can add meaningful value.

Traditional Plan

The Traditional Plan generally has higher employee premiums but may offer a more familiar structure with lower deductible exposure and copays for certain services.

  • More predictable costs at point of care.
  • Higher paycheck deductions.
  • Compatible with a full-purpose Health Care FSA.
What is an HSA?

A Health Savings Account, or HSA, is a tax-advantaged account used to pay eligible medical expenses. Funds can carry over from year to year and the account is owned by the employee.

What is a Health Care FSA?

A Health Care Flexible Spending Account allows employees to set aside pre-tax money for eligible health care expenses. FSAs are usually plan-year based and may have use-it-or-lose-it rules.

What is a Limited Purpose FSA?

A Limited Purpose FSA is generally used for eligible dental and vision expenses and may be paired with an HSA-compatible medical plan, depending on plan rules.

Why Total Compensation Matters

Total compensation includes more than salary. Employer-paid contributions toward benefits, HSA funding, retirement matching, leave, holidays, and other resources add value to the employment package.

  • Personal days are use-it-or-lose-it and do not roll over.

Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA)

Eligible employees may qualify for up to 12 weeks of unpaid, job-protected leave during a 12-month rolling look-back period.

  • Eligibility generally requires 12 months of employment.
  • Employees must have physically worked at least 1,250 hours during the 12 months before leave begins.
  • Leave may be unpaid, paid using available leave, or a combination.

Employee Assistance Program (EAP)

The EAP provides confidential support for employees and household members for personal, family, financial, and work-life concerns.

Tuition Assistance

James City County offers tuition assistance with a maximum benefit of $5,250 per person per calendar year.

Recreation & Community Access

  • Free access to the James City County Recreation Center.
  • Discounted family membership at the JCC Recreation Center.
  • Free access to the Abram Fink Community Center.
  • Family access to outdoor pools, Jamestown Beach, and special events.

Additional Financial & Family Benefits

  • Public Service Loan Forgiveness: James City County qualifies as a public service organization.
  • Employee Homeownership Program: Eligible employees purchasing a primary residence in James City County or the City of Williamsburg may receive a dollar-for-dollar savings match up to $3,000.
  • Virginia529: Employees may access Virginia529 education savings options. This benefit is not payroll deducted.
